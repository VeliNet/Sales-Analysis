# Sales-Analysis
A comprehensive performance analysis of sales operations across regions, products, and customer segments. It tracks actual performance against projected targets, highlights profitability trends, and enables interactive filtering by segment.

**PROBLEM STATEMENT** 
There is profit loss even when there is revenue, discounts and high performance in sales from segments. Our goal is to maximise profits in the business while sustaining growth, discounts, products and segments performance varying across regions and time. So, we need to know what/ who is driving performance and when the performance is increasing and dropping

**DATA CLEANING** 
The following steps were taken to prepare, clean and transform the data;
•	We removed duplicates from the employee id column in the facts table 
•	Assigned the right data types to each of the columns 
•	Removed all the null values from the different columns in the facts table
•	Used the DAX function to derive columns and measures like profits, total sales and total profits, total units sold, total sales, grade, projected sales and projected profits, quarter, month and year 

**KEY INSIGHTS** 
•	Sales performance / revenue increased within the Q3 and Q4 
•	Profit margins differ widely by the product and discount band 
•	Not all high selling products makes profits 
•	Higher discounts increased units sold 
•	Certain segments outperform the others in sales and profits 

**TOOLS AND TECHNIQUES USED**
Power BI and Excel were used for analysis and dashboards. Key techniques include data cleaning, trend analysis, comparison of segment and peak periods in performance in profit margins, revenue, discounts efficiency 

**RECOMMENDATIONS**
•	Focus on high selling products with moderate discounts and plan for early inventory 
•	Phase out the weak performing products after reviewing the cost of production, units sold and other pricing strategies 
•	Q3 AND Q4 are the very strong selling periods. There should be planned inventory ahead of time and running of early campaign
•	The Q1 and Q2 should be used for testing, review and cost optimisation 
•	There should be more allocation of budget and selling efforts to the top segments making the most sales and profits 
•	Reward the salesperson with the highest sales 
•	Implement discounts by segments and the products
